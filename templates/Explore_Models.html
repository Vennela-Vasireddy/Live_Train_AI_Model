UNDERSTAND THE PROJECT

Why CNN?

They're great at recognizing patterns in images, videos, or even sounds. CNN stands for Convolutional Neural Network. Here's a breakdown for you:

Think like a detective: The CNN is like a detective looking at an image, say, of a cat. It doesn't see the whole picture at once. Instead, it breaks the image into smaller squares, like looking at the cat's fur, eyes, or paws one at a time.

Finding patterns: In each square, the CNN searches for specific patterns, like edges, curves, or blobs. These patterns are like fingerprints for the cat's features.

Building a bigger picture: Once it has identified patterns in each square, the CNN puts them all together to understand the whole image. It's like the detective assembling clues to identify the cat.

What are Convolutional layers?

These are the layers in a CNN, where the pattern recognition happens. Each layer uses filters to find specific patterns in the image. Imagine the filters as different magnifying glasses, each looking for something specific. There could be multiple layers, each building on the last to create a more complex understanding of the image.



How many layers are needed?



That depends on the project! The number of layers determines how intricate the CNN's analysis is.



Think of it this way: More layers are like having a more powerful detective team with more magnifying glasses to uncover even the tiniest details in the image.
